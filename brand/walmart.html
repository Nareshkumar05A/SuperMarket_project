Walmart Inc. is an American multinational retail corporation that operates a chain of hypermarkets, discount department stores, and grocery stores. It was founded by Sam Walton on July 2, 1962, in Rogers, Arkansas. Headquartered in Bentonville, Arkansas, Walmart is the world's largest company by revenue and one of the biggest private employers globally.

Over the years, Walmart has expanded its reach through Walmart.com, Sam's Club, and its acquisition of international operations. Walmart also competes in the e-commerce space and is continuously enhancing its online services to rival major digital marketplaces. The company is known for its "Everyday Low Prices" philosophy and commitment to customer value.

COVID-19

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Walmart played a critical role by keeping essential goods available to the public. The company implemented strict safety protocols in stores, expanded contactless delivery and pickup options, and hired over 400,000 employees to meet increased demand. Walmart also launched health initiatives, including in-store testing and vaccination sites, and supported communities through donations and relief programs.
